Topic 1

📚 Handling Exam Stress

Exams feel scary because your brain doesn't know the difference between danger and exams. Here's how to calm it down.

01
Use the Pomodoro Technique ⏱️
Study for exactly 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After 4 sessions, take a 20-minute break. Your brain learns better in short bursts than long hours.
02
Make a Study Plan 📅
Write down all your subjects and topics. Spread them over available days. When you see a plan, your brain feels less overwhelmed — like untangling a messy knot.
03
Teach What You Learn 🎓
Explain a topic to your imaginary student (or a friend). If you can explain it simply, you truly understand it. This method is called the Feynman Technique.
04
Sleep is NOT Optional 😴
Your brain sorts and stores memories while you sleep. Studying all night and sleeping 3 hours is worse than studying less and sleeping 8 hours. Sleep hard to study smart.
05
5-5-5 Breathing Before an Exam 🫁
Breathe in slowly for 5 counts. Hold for 5 counts. Breathe out for 5 counts. Do this 3 times before entering the exam hall. It activates your calm nervous system instantly.
06
Stop Comparing Your Preparation 🚫
When friends say "I haven't studied anything!" they're probably not telling the truth. Your journey is your own. Focus on what YOU know, not what others claim to know.
Topic 2

🫂 Overcoming Social Anxiety

Social anxiety gets smaller the more you face it — slowly and safely. Here's how to start.

💙 Key idea: Don't try to avoid anxiety — that makes it bigger. Instead, face small fears one at a time. Each time you do, the fear gets 10% smaller.
🧗
Start Small & Scale Up

Make a "fear ladder." Write 10 social situations from least scary to most scary. Start with the easiest one this week. Slowly move up the ladder.

🎭
The "Interested Person" Trick

In social situations, focus on the OTHER person. Ask them questions. Be curious about them. This takes the spotlight off you and makes conversations flow naturally.

🗣️
Practice "Safe" Social Situations

Join a club, class, or online group where everyone shares an interest. Talking about something you both love makes socialising 10x easier.

✓
Challenge Your Thoughts 🧠
When you think "everyone is judging me," ask yourself: Is this actually true? What's the evidence? Usually, people are too busy thinking about themselves to judge you.
✓
Use Your Body Language 💃
Stand straight. Make eye contact. Smile first. Your body language doesn't just show confidence — it actually creates it. Try the "power pose" for 2 minutes before scary situations.
Topic 3

💪 Building Confidence

Confidence is built through small wins. The more you act confident, the more confident you actually become.

🌱 The Confidence Challenge

Week 1: Do one thing that scares you a little
Like answering a question in class or starting a conversation
Week 2: Write 3 things you're proud of daily
Even small wins count: "I got ready on time today"
Week 3: Help someone else
Helping others makes YOU feel more capable and valued
Week 4: Share your opinion in a group
Your thoughts and ideas matter. Practice voicing them.
💡
Stop Following Accounts That Make You Feel Bad
If you always feel worse after seeing someone's posts, unfollow them. Your mental health is more important than staying "updated."
💡
Focus on Progress, Not Perfection
Nobody is perfect. Not even the people you admire. Celebrate getting 1% better every day. In a year, you'll be 37x better.
💡
Find Your "Thing" 🎯
Play a sport, learn guitar, draw, code, cook — anything you can get better at. Having a skill builds real confidence because it proves you CAN achieve things.
💡
Use Positive Self-Talk Daily 🗣️
Every morning, say one kind thing to yourself. "I am capable." "I am enough." "I will do my best today." Sounds cheesy, but it literally rewires your brain over time.
Topic 4

😤 Managing Anger

Anger isn't the enemy. Uncontrolled anger is. Learn to use it without it using you.

🔟
Count to 10

When you feel your anger rising, count slowly to 10 before you say or do anything. This tiny pause gives your rational brain time to catch up with your emotional brain.

🏃
Move Your Body

Anger creates energy in your body. Release it physically — run, do push-ups, hit a pillow, dance wildly. Physical activity burns off the stress chemicals that anger creates.

📓
Anger Journal

Write down what made you angry, how your body felt, and what you did. Patterns will appear. You'll discover your triggers and you can prepare for them next time.

💬
Use "I" Statements

Instead of "You always ignore me!" say "I feel hurt when I don't feel heard." "I" statements express your feelings without attacking others — fewer arguments, more solutions.

🎵
Music & Art

Listen to music that matches and then slowly calms your mood. Draw, paint, or create something with your anger energy. Art transforms feelings into something beautiful.

🤝
Forgiveness

Holding onto anger hurts you more than the other person. Forgiving doesn't mean what they did was okay — it means you're choosing peace for YOURSELF.

Topic 5

📱 Managing Screen Time

You don't have to quit your phone. You just need to be the boss of it — not the other way around.

📱 Your Phone Detox Plan — Start Anywhere
☀️
No Phone in Bed
Keep your phone outside your bedroom. Use an alarm clock instead.
🍽️
No Phone at Meals
Put it face down during meals. Be present with the food and people around you.
📵
App Time Limits
Use your phone's Screen Time / Digital Wellbeing settings to set daily limits.
📵
Delete Apps That Drain You
Which app do you open when you're bored but feel worse after using? Delete or hide it for 30 days and notice the difference.
🌿
Replace Screen Time with Real Time
When you feel the urge to scroll, do 10 minutes of something real: walk outside, call a friend, read 5 pages, or stretch. Build real-life dopamine.
Topic 6

😴 Better Sleep Tips

Bad sleep makes everything worse — stress, anxiety, anger, and even confidence. Fix your sleep, fix your life.

😴
Sleep and Wake at the Same Time
Even on weekends. Your body has an internal clock. Irregular sleep schedules confuse it and lead to worse sleep quality.
📵
No Screens 30 Min Before Bed
Blue light from screens tricks your brain into thinking it's daytime. Read a book, listen to calm music, or just lie quietly instead.
🌡️
Keep Your Room Cool & Dark
Your body temperature drops to initiate sleep. A cool, dark room helps this happen faster and gives you deeper, more restful sleep.
